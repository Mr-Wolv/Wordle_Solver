      HOW TO PLAY

      This is a strategy assistant for the real Wordle. You play Wordle as usual in your browser; this app tells you the smartest next guess and lets you enter the result. It never plays for you — you decide every word.

      1. The goal: guess the hidden 5-letter word in 6 tries. After each guess, Wordle colors the tiles: correct letter & spot, correct letter, wrong spot, not in the word.
      2. Read the three columns: SOLVE lists the best next guesses (ranked by info gain + win chance); SHRED lists the remaining answers with the highest chance of being the secret (its P(ans) posterior — the most likely words still in play). POOL (bottom-left) is every word still possible.
      3. Enter your guess: type on your keyboard or click a word in SOLVE / SHRED to drop it onto the board. Then press Enter (or the green ENTER) to submit.
      4. Report the colors: click each tile on the active row to cycle absent → present → correct, matching what Wordle showed you. The candidate pool shrinks automatically.
      5. Repeat until solved (the board shows SOLVED ✓) or you run out of rows. Hit SYSTEM RESET any time to start a fresh game.
      6. HARD MODE (optional): forces every guess to use letters already confirmed present — the real Wordle hard rule. It locks on after turn 1.
      7. NYT HINT (optional): reveals a letter the answer contains — 0, 1, or 2 hints, your call (mode locks after turn 1). 0 hints: pure deduction (already closes 100%). 1 hint: any single letter you pick. 2 hints: the NYT rule forces exactly one vowel + one consonant — it will never accept two of the same class, so a 2-hint game always has a known vowel and a known consonant. Note: once a hint is taken it can only narrow; some words simply have no second qualifying letter to reveal, which is why 0/1 are valid choices too. Not required — a hint can just shave a turn.
      8. Search POOL: type letters to filter the candidate list (matches are highlighted).
        • plain letters (any count) → words containing all those letters, any order: cr finds CRATE, TRACE, RANCH (each has C and R).
        • _ = wildcard → positional pattern: a___e matches A_C_E; a____ matches words starting with A.
        POOL is read-only reference — it highlights matches but never loads a guess; loading happens from SOLVE / SHRED.
